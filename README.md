This project is about understanding the role of climate change in crop yields in west Africa. It aligns with SDG 2 (Zero hunger) and SDG 13 (Climate action). We attempted to visualize how changing average temperatures and annual temperature are creating an impact on the production of African staple foods. 

In this project, we have demonstrated the effect of rainfall, temperature and other climatic factors on the year-over-Year (YoY) yield growth and production of three (3) staple crops namely cassava, Maize, Rice and Sorghum in 5 west-African countries namely, Nigeria, Ghana, Cote D’Ivoire, Senegal, and Mali between the year 2020 – 2024.
